## The map nobody gave you

A few things about the working world that everyone assumes you know, and nobody actually tells you:

- **Companies are split into functions.** Every organisation — a startup, a consultancy, a tech giant — divides into teams that build the thing (product, engineering), sell it (sales, marketing), run the machine (operations, finance, people), and steer it (strategy, leadership). You will belong to one of these functions, and "what do you do?" means "which function, at what level?"
- **Jobs have titles, and titles are searchable.** Nobody advertises for "a philosopher". They advertise for *analysts*, *associates*, *coordinators*, *researchers*, *consultants*. Half the battle is learning which titles hide work you'd love — each guide below lists them.
- **Careers are chains, not choices.** Almost nobody is doing the job they'll retire from. Your first role is a way in, not a verdict: two years somewhere reasonable buys you skills, a network, and much better options. Choosing "wrong" costs far less than choosing nothing.
- **Your degree maps to skills, not to a title.** Analysis, writing, argument, honest updating under pressure — every guide on this site is just a different market for the same toolkit.

## Or walk the map yourself

Ten fields, in plain language. "You'll like it if…" is doing the real work in each one.

### [AI & Data Ethics]({{< ref "paths/ai-data-ethics" >}})
Tech companies, auditors and institutes hiring people to reason about fairness and harm in AI systems. Roles are called *AI ethics researcher, responsible AI analyst, AI governance manager*. You'll like it if you want your ethics training pointed at the decade's defining technology.

### [Ethics Consulting]({{< ref "paths/ethics-consulting" >}})
Small firms and university centres paid for ethical judgement itself. Roles: *ethics consultant, research governance adviser*. You'll like it if you want the job closest to the seminar room — real cases instead of thought experiments.

### [Management Consulting]({{< ref "paths/consulting" >}})
The classic route: firms like the Big Four and McKinsey hire graduate generalists to solve business problems in small teams. Roles: *business analyst, associate consultant*. You'll like it if you want structure, training and a prestigious first chapter that opens every later door.

### [Risk & Catastrophic Risk]({{< ref "paths/risk" >}})
Tech-platform teams preventing harm at scale, and research institutes working on the biggest threats. Roles: *risk analyst, trust & safety analyst, researcher*. You'll like it if reasoning about uncertainty and worst cases feels like home.

### [Blockchain & Fintech]({{< ref "paths/blockchain-fintech" >}})
Young companies building financial and governance systems from scratch. Roles: *protocol researcher, governance analyst, community lead*. You'll like it if designing institutions sounds better than joining one.

### [Venture Capital]({{< ref "paths/venture-capital" >}})
Small funds betting on early companies. Roles: *venture analyst, investment associate*. You'll like it if you enjoy defending a controversial thesis — an investment memo is exactly that.

### [Entrepreneurship & Startups]({{< ref "paths/entrepreneurship-startups" >}})
Founding something, or joining a five-person team where you do everything. Roles: *founder, founding team, chief of staff, generalist*. You'll like it if ownership beats stability and you'd rather build the option than pick one.

### [Media & Writing]({{< ref "paths/media-writing" >}})
Content marketing, technical writing, journalism, public philosophy. Roles: *content writer, copywriter, editor*. You'll like it if the writing was your favourite part of the degree.

### [Cybersecurity & Recruitment]({{< ref "paths/cybersecurity-recruitment" >}})
Judging and connecting technical experts without being one. Roles: *recruitment consultant, talent acquisition specialist*. You'll like it if you read people as carefully as you read arguments.

### [Business, Mentoring & Beyond]({{< ref "paths/business-mentoring" >}})
The generalist roles every organisation runs on — operations, programs, people, ESG — plus mission-driven mentoring orgs. Roles: *operations associate, program coordinator, chief of staff*. You'll like it if you want to keep your options open inside organisations you believe in.

## And this is still just a subset

These ten guides are the journeys our community happens to have taken — not the boundary of what's possible. Philosophers we know have also gone into **law, public policy, government, teaching, product management, publishing and the civil service**, and plenty of places we haven't written up yet. If you've walked a path that's missing here, [tell us your story](/#share-your-story) — the next lost graduate needs it.
